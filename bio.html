biography

Alexandria O’Neal Johnson is a multidisciplinary artist and performer from Dallas, TX. She began dancing at age 6 in the West African drum and dance ensemble, Djeli Kunda, directed by Moussa Diabate. Alexandria toured Canada with Les Amazones de Guinea as a guest artist before beginning her formal training at 14 on scholarship at Dallas Black Dance Academy. As a member of the Academy’s Junior Performing Ensemble, BLOOM, directed by Nycole Ray, she performed in Kampala, Uganda in the Kwetu (Peace) Festival. She was also an apprentice with Dallas Black Dance Theater II.


Alexandria has had the opportunity to work with choreographers such as Jennifer Archibald, Hope Boykin, Clifton Brown, Hope Clarke, Ronald K. Brown, Earl Mosley, and Darrell Moultrie. She has also worked with Francesca Harper in The Francesca Harper Project, and as an assistant and associate to choreographer Matthew Rushing. Alexandria works with Brooklyn based band, Lady Moon & The Eclipse as both choreographer and dancer.


As a member of JUNTOS Collective, she helped to facilitate dance workshops in Spanish and performed in Costa Rica and Nicaragua. Alexandria has taught for Richmond Public Schools, Dance Theatre of Harlem, The Ailey School, Dallas Black Dance Academy, City University of New York, and been a Guest Teaching Artist at Pratt Institute. She has also taught for dance intensives in the United States, United Kingdom, and South Africa. She is a certified Zena Rommett Floor Barre® Technique Instructor.


She has recorded with PoemTalk at University of Pennsylvania, presented an interdisciplinary art installation titled, "Libation," at Pratt Institute, modeled for the School of Visual Art, and directed and choreographed a one woman show in collaboration with poet, Candice Iloh. She has also appeared on PBS’s “Sesame Street” for West African dance.


Alexandria is a graduate of the Alvin Ailey/Fordham University BFA program in Dance, and holds an MFA in Performance and Performance Studies from Pratt Institute.
